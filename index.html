Society garlic: African Zulu people using it for respiratory issues, colds, and intestinal worms, while Asian and European traditions utilize it for blood pressure, rheumatism, and digestive problems

Pomegranate: originating in the Middle East and used in ancient diets and medicinal practices for thousands of years, with various cultures associating them with fertility, abundance, good fortune, and rebirth

Trailing lantana: it has a history in folk medicine, but its primary traditional use is as an ornamental plant and for attracting wildlife

Lantana: Traditional applications include teas for colds and fevers, poultices for skin issues, and tonics for respiratory problems. Its aromatic leaves are used as insect repellents, and the ripe berries can be made into jams and beverages in some cultures.

Moses in the cradle: In traditional medicine, extracts from the plant have been used to treat various ailments, including colds, coughs, and sore throats.
